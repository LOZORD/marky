About the Office

Dr. Rudberg is an out-of-network provider. Our office will submit claims to your insurance company as a courtesy. Patients are responsible for payment at the time of service.

Dr. Rudberg has opted out of the Medicare program and neither our office nor our patients can submit claims to Medicare. This applies to the doctor's services only. If you are sent for testing at another facility depending on their policies, that should fall back under the Medicare program.

Q

A

Q

How long are office visits?

A

A new patient office visit is a 1 ½ hour appointment.

A yearly physical visit is a 1 hour appointment.

An established follow-up or sick visit is a ½ hour appointment.

Q

Is the cost of the visit charged by the length of the visit?

A

The cost is based on the complexity of the visit. New patient and physicals do cost a bit more.

Education

After his undergrad at UW-Milwaukee, Dr. Rudberg earned his Doctor of Medicine degree from the Medical College of Wisconsin, where he was elected into AOA, a national honors society. Dr. Rudberg then completed his training in Internal Medicine at Boston University Medical Center. Next, he completed a fellowship in Geriatrics and received a Masters of Public Health, both at B.U.

Research and Teaching

After training, Dr. Rudberg became a full-time faculty member at the University of Chicago with the Section of General Internal Medicine. There his patient care, in a variety of settings, focused on both Geriatrics and General Internal Medicine of younger adults. He was an attendant on the General Internal Consultative Service and the General Inpatient Units.

Among his many teaching activities, he was the Fellowship Director of Geriatrics, a member of the Committee of Nutritional Biology and Human Nutrition, and the Medical Director of the teaching nursing home of the University of Chicago. Dr. Rudberg's primary research interest is to develop ways to maintain independence for frail older persons. He has published over 40 articles on dementia, falls, incontinence, delirium, nutrition, and exercise. In 1987, he was promoted to a senior faculty member of the University of Chicago.

National and International Work

Dr. Rudberg is an international lecturer on the ethical and clinical challenges facing both families and physicians. He has presented a paper to the United States Senate on ways to improve care of older persons. He has been a member of several national working groups such as the Ethics Committee of the American Medical Directors Association, and a member of the Mentoring Group of the Society of General Internal Medicine.

Recent Work

Since 1998, Dr. Rudberg has been on staff at Lake Forest Hospital. Through his affiliations there, he was asked to serve on Congressman Mark Kirk's Healthcare Taskforce Advisory Board, and again on a similar board when Kirk became a senator in 2010. Among his many interests, Dr. Rudberg is an avid cyclist, swimmer, and traveler. He is also a coffee enthusiast, and occasional indulger in funfetti cakes.
